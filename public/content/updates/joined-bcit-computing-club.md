## BCIT Computing Club and Coding 4 All

The BCIT Computing Club (CC) and Coding 4 All (C4A) offer valuable guidance, events, and opportunities for students interested in computing.

Together, these clubs help support the BCIT computing community through initiatives such as hackathons, midterm and final review sessions, LeetCode nights, and other student-focused events.

## Work

Other executives and I took the time to bundle, rework, and host CST review sessions for Term 2 student midterms, while also planning for final exam reviews. Each of us focused on a specific course and helped prepare example questions, quizzes, and guidance for larger student groups.

Additionally, we volunteered at Hack the Break (March 13–15, 2026), helping with setup, teardown, and general event staffing.

## Why it mattered to me

Before officially joining the BCIT Computing Club and Coding 4 All, I had already attended review sessions led by friends who were also executives. During those sessions, I was able to help answer questions both one-on-one and for the larger audience. Hearing more about the work that went into organizing events like HTTPHacks also made it clear to me that this was something I wanted to be part of.

Joining these clubs gave me the opportunity to contribute more directly to a community that supports students in practical ways, while also helping me learn valuable lessons through volunteering, coordination, event setup, and guiding students to do their best.
